June Freebie // Vintage Glasses Pattern Weekly Planner & Wallpapers

Published // 30 May, 2016  |  Column // Freebie

Hi all! I have another fun little freebie for you. Planners really seem to be a thing at the moment, especially within my orbit of bloggers and creatives.

When you have so much to do in daily life, keeping reminders in your head is of no productive help. You need to get them out, to let your mind relax and let it drift onto more important matters than remembering the 101 things you feel you need to get done. Purging all that from our heads onto paper is important.

And you might as well do so on something that is pleasing to the eye as well as useful.

So I’ve created a little weekly planner for you all to download. I loved drawing the big vintage-style glasses on my portrait illustration Minerva, so I thought glasses would be a good theme. And I’ve wanted to try my hand at a pattern for a while, so I thought "why not give it a shot here"; my freebies aren’t just a present for you, but a place where I can play about with ideas without wondering if they a sellable/marketable.

  • Download the Vintage Glasses Desk Weekly Planner

And I’m really chuffed with the result. So much so that I’m not just giving away the printable weekly planner, but even created a few additional freebie wallpapers for you as well!

Wish List // 6 Creations by Independent Makers Who Celebrate Feminism and Gender Equality

Published // 25 May, 2016  |  Column // Wish list

There is little in the political sphere that I care more about than feminism, and it’s heartening to see so many makers create items that celebrate the fight for equality. Here I’ve rounded up a few of my favourite items, made by small creative businesses, to support the feminist cause.

1. Feminism is Cool Iron-on Embroidered Patch

With the tagline "Wearables for feminists, badass babes, + witchy weirdos", all of Midge Blitz creations focus on feminism and equality issues. This patch would be the perfect addition to a bag or jacket.

2. Feminist with a To-Do List Enamel Lapel Pin

A great lapel pin for any women out there fighting the good fight. I love the simplicity of this square gold and black pin.

3. Birth Control Pills Enamel Pin

What better way to celebrate female liberation than with a lapel pin of one of the things that helped us gain so much of our freedom?

4. Feminist Killjoy Vinyl Sticker Sheet

I adore these vibrant and playful Feminist Killjoy stickers, with some great sayings and tag lines.

5. Support Your Babes Enamel

Women should not be competing with, but instead supporting each other. $1 from every purchase from Strange Women Society goes directly to a woman-centric aid organisation.

6. Smash The Patriarchy Floral Hand Embroidery Hoop Art

So pretty, and oh so powerful. Rather than rejecting supposed girly things, this gorgeous piece of embroidery brings it together with the message of equality.

My Work // Illustration of Haruki Murakami's novel Sputnik Sweetheart

Published // 23 May, 2016  |  Column // My work

Here is a new illustration in my line of pieces devoted to Haruki Murakami novels. This time it is of Sputnik Sweetheart, a tale about one man’s search for a friend who has mysteriously disappeared while in Greece.

The novel is largely about how we mould ourselves, losing who we truly are, in order to adapt and live in a conformist society. It’s another Murakami novel I remember loving, however as it’s been, I think, seven years since I’ve read it, a lot of the detail and its events have left my memory. I’m looking forward to rereading this novel on my chronological reread of all of Murakami’s novels.

As I’ve mentioned before when sharing my other Haruki Murakami illustrations, a few years ago I decided to do an illustration for each of Haruki Murakami’s novels in order to practice. Each was hand drawn in pencil and coloured digitally in Photoshop. I was surprised by how popular they became, and had many people asking if any of them were for sale as prints. But I wasn’t quite happy enough with them to have them printed; I was at a point where I was still deep into training and hadn’t yet developed a style I was settled on.

Buy this Piece on Society6, Art Rookie,
Redbubble or Etsy

Two and a half years went by. I was now working full time as an illustrator and designer, my skills had developed fourfold, and I had a look and feel I was delighted with. So I felt that maybe it was time to recreate the whole project and finally allow fellow Murakami fans to purchase them as prints and other products.

So here is my new version of the illustration for Murakami’s novel, Sputnik Sweetheart. This illustration is of, unsurprisingly, the satellite Sputnik, set against a dark but enchanting spacescape (is that a word?). I think it works well as a kind of dreamy image, the star and nebula filled background softening the cold, hard edge of the metallic spacecraft.

As normal, I have released it as an A6 and A4 print on my Etsy store. If you go for the A6 art print (as shown above), you can choose to add a few of my other Murkamai illustrations to the order to save a little money on each.

This illustration is also available as a range of products over on Art Rookie, Redbubble and Society6.

You can also get a sneak peek at all the rest of my Haruki Murakami novel illustrations over on my portfolio.
