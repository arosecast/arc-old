Loved Links // 6 Fun and Quirky Valentine's Day Free Printables and DIYs

Published // 12 February, 2016  |  Column // Loved links

My partner and I have never really celebrated Valentine's much. His birthday is just over two weeks before it, my birthday is THREE days before, and our anniversary takes place a month after.

But I’ve seen so many fun and creative DIYs and freebies floating around lately that I though more than a few of them are worth doing.

1. Printable David Bowie Valentines

Really any David Bowie related printable is a-okay in my books, so if you want to honour the musical genius this Valentine's Day, head over to The House that Lars Built and get these free Bowie printable cards.

2. Printable 90s Rap Lyric Valentines

Or if your musical tastes are more 90s rap orientated, Almost Makes Perfect has these pretty but cheeky calligraphy Valentine Day's cards using quotes from some fondly remembered rap songs.

3. Parks & Rec Galentine’s Day Printables

If you're more of a Galentine's fan, or just love a bit of Parks and Recreation, you can't go wrong with these beautifully done Galentine's Day cards from Brit + Co. Leslie Knope is probably the best friend a girl could ask for, so giving a card to your bestie with one of her quotes is perfectly fitting.

4. Printable Funny Valentines

These humourous pastel coloured Valentine Day cards from Sugar and Cloth are incredibly cute. I'm sure it no surprise I'm especially loving the cat themed one.

5. Heart Eye Emoji Patterned Cake Roll

I've been wanting to try my hand at making a patterned cake roll for a while now, and this heart-themed one from Aww, Sam would go down perfectly for Valentine's. And you can't have any holiday without cake being involved in some way or another.

6. DIY Concrete Copper Heart Candles

These cute homemade concrete and copper heart candles from I Spy DIY would be a great way to decorate the home for the holiday, and inject a little romance as well. I really am loving just about everything copper at the moment (like probably everyone else).

My Work // Crazy Cat Lady | Wooden Brooch of a Girl and Her Cats

Published // 9 February, 2016  |  Column // My work

I've now released a few little pieces of illustrated jewellery. I have two rose gold and walnut wood portrait pendant necklaces, and a cat and flower themed brooch.

Today I’m adding another cute cat brooch; an ode to all the crazy cat ladies out there (like myself).

This was actually the first piece I designed for a non-print product, and it inspired me to create the Wild Sofa Beasts product line. This piece includes all of my three cats, Mog, Toby and Monty. As with almost all of my pieces, this was painted in watercolour, but this time watercolour was used for the black outer lines as well, instead of my normal pencil.

Buy this Piece on Society6 or Etsy

Soon Wild Sofa Beasts will not solely be comprised of wooden brooches. I’m hoping to not only create cat-themed prints, brooches and other pieces of jewellery under this label, but also stickers, stationery and maybe eventually patterned fabric.

The last of my finished cat-themed wooden brooches, a book-themed one, can be seen over on my portfolio and will be released very soon. And I’m currently working on more brooches, including one for all you dog lovers out there.

Freebie // February 2016 - Downloadable 'Take a Leap' Printable

Published // 2 February, 2016  |  Column // Freebie

Last month I created some cat and celebration themed digital wallpapers for you all. For this month's freebie, I decided to make an illustrated printable.

As Leap Day only comes around once every four years, I thought I should take this opportunity to make something devoted to that rare day and the meaning behind it.

Writing down a goal or dream helps us move towards achieving it. So I thought why not create something pretty to use in this way? And this little freebie, with its leaping woman (her strawberry tights hint at 27th February being National Strawberry Day) and calligraphic lettering, was born.

  • Download the ‘Take a Leap’ Printable (PDF)

All that is needed is a printer, some thick card stock (even paper is grand), and a pen. There are three different colour versions, so print out your favourite one (or, well, all of them), write down what big chance you want to take this month, and stick the card up on your fridge, pin board, or somewhere else in sight.

Enjoy!

Wish List // 6 Cute and Wonderful Items for Cat Lovers

Published // 28 January, 2016  |  Column // Wish list

I think if you asked any of my family or friends what my most loved thing in creation was, they would waste no time before saying cats.

I love pretty much all animals, but these little feline rascals manage to push ahead of the pack, and something cat-related will always pop up in my Christmas and birthday presents. So here are a few of by favourite kitty-themed items created my independent artists.

1. Avocatdo Soft Enamel Pin

I love cats and I love avocado. So a pin that combines them both? Pony People has so many wonderful cat-related items, and I’m having a hard time choosing between them. But this one is maybe at the top of my list.

2. I Like CATS

Anything from the Etsy store I Like CATS, run by Toby, is worth a purchase. Pins, prints, t-shirts, tote bags ... the list goes on. Everything is equally adorable and cat themed.

3. 'There Better Be Cats' Tombstone Enamel Pin

Another pin, but I couldn’t help myself. A tombstone all about cats. Perfect. And for all you dog owners out there, there is a dog version of this pin as well.

4. Rose Gold & White Diamond Kitty Face Necklace

Not cheap, but then the cat’s ears are adorned with diamonds, so you can’t really complain. If you’re looking for something a little more than fashion jewellery, then this delicate necklace is worth the money.

5. Hand Embroidered Cat (or Dog) Portrait

There are a fair number of artists and illustrators on Etsy creating beautiful custom pet portraits. But an embroidered one is something maybe a little more special and rare.

6. I'm So Pretty Cat & Flower Wooden Brooch

And last, but hopefully not least, one of my newest products. This is an illustrated wooden brooch produced for my new Wild Sofa Beasts line, products inspired by my own cats. You can see a few other future pieces on my folio, which will be avaiable soon!
