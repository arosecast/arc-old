Creative Spotlight // 3 Watercolor Illustrators You Should Be Following

Published // 14 January, 2016  |  Column // Creative spotlight

As my work is primarily created using watercolours, I'm always looking for other artists who work within the same medium to glean new ideas and find inspiration. Here are a few who have captured my attention recently.

1. Helen Green

If you are a fan of either David Bowie or Lady Gaga, I am sure you’ve come upon Helen Green’s illustration work before. She focuses on portraiture, particularly related to the music and fashion industries; her David Bowie career-spanning time lapse animation is a sight to see and especially poignant this week. Her style is wonderfully unique; despite its realistic appearance, you can always tell her pieces from other watercolour artists. I also love her pattern illustration designs, such as these ones of Red Dwarf and Duran Duran.

2. Megan Lynn Kott

Cats and watercolour: always a pleasing combination in my mind. A lifelong cat lover, Megan Lynn Kott is not only an amazing watercolour illustrator, but has an often subversive sense of humour that makes her pieces standout. She has worked with clients including Old Navy, Chronicle Books, Gymboree, and Tea Collection, and has realised a book full of temporary cat tattoos, aptly named Cattoos!.

3. Marion Bolognesi

Marion Bolognesi creates expressive, abstract portraiture where the eyes truely are the window to the soul. I love how free she is with her painting; not only are the facial features so lifelike, but they are melded with gorgeous spots and splashes of intense colour.

My Work // I Feel Pretty | Wooden Brooch of a Ginger Cat with Flowers

Published // 12 January, 2016  |  Column // My work

A few weeks ago I released my first pieces of illustrated jewellery; two rose gold and walnut wood pendant necklaces. And today I'm adding this cat-themed wooden brooch to my product line.

This piece is based on Toby, my rather handsome part-Norwegian Forest ginger tom cat. It’s a bit of a thing at the moment, portraits of animals and people surrounded by flowers, so I thought I’d try my hand at a slightly tongue-in-cheek version of this.

Buy this Piece on Society6 or Etsy

As with almost all of my pieces, this was painted in watercolour, but this time watercolour was used for the black outer lines as well, instead of my normal pencil.

‘I Feel Pretty’ is the first of a product line I’ve started called ‘Wild Sofa Beasts’. I’m hoping to not only create cat-themed prints, brooches and other pieces of jewellery under this label, but also stickers, stationery and maybe eventually patterned fabric.

You can have a sneak peek at the other two finished cat-themed wooden brooches over on my portfolio, (which include my other two cats Mog and Monty). For now, ‘I Feel Pretty’ is the only one available to purchase, but I’m hoping to add a new one every month to my online stores.

UPDATE: I have now added a new Wild Sofa Beasts wooden brooch, Crazy Cat Lady.

Loved Links // 6 Downloadable Freebies to Help Cure the January Blues

Published // 7 January, 2016  |  Column // Loved links

Well, that’s the holidays over for another year. By now most of us are back at work. The days are still horribly short and weather is getting a bit colder.

To top off all these normal new year woes, on my first day back to work (literally at 9am), I slipped on some wet, tiled stairs and now have a lovely set of bright bruises all down my left side! Blurgh!

I think we all need a little pick me up to get over the January blues. So I’m sharing a few New Year / January 2016 downloadable freebies to cheer us all up.

1. Gold and Black New Year’s Printable 2016 Calendar

I love the sophistication of Cocorrina's free downloadable 2016 monthly calendar. Beautiful rough, hand-written month titles created in black marker pen, with gold 2016 stamps, and a simple clean calendar below. Great for a printable as it won’t use too much ink.

2. Printable 2016 Monthly and Weekly Planner

If I don’t plan things out, I find it hard to figure out what I’m supposed to be doing on a day-to-day basis. With all the hopes and resolutions that come with the new year, it’s the perfect time for a lovely and pretty planner. With lovely calligraphy titles and plenty of room for your plans, these two downloadable printables - a weekly and a monthly planner - are free over on the Small Paper Things blog.

5. Printable Thank You Card

I only know one or two people who still routinely send out thank you cards; I know it’s never been something I’ve done, normally saying thank you at the time when receiving a present, or sending the person a text message. But it is a nice tradition, and a lovely way spread some cheer in a physical way. Although originally created for Thanksgiving, the cuteness of this free downloadable card, by Teela of Every Tuesday, is a great excuse to send some caring snail mail.

4. Free Printable 2016 Coloring Calendar of Ridiculous Holidays

Ridiculous holidays are big at the moment. My planner even has a few pages dedicated to them. So how about free printable calendar pages that not only celebrate these fun holidays, but that you can colour in yourself? A collaboration between the blog Damask Love and illustrator Kristen Magee, this is a great wee freebie to help you relax in through the rest of 2016.

3. Digital Wallpapers of Cakes with Motivating Messages

Cake makes everything better. So how about digital wallpapers of cakes with motivating messages? DesignLoveFest always has a great selection of digital wallpapers, and I love the quirky and fun vibe behind these photographic ones.

6. Cat-Themed Celebration Wallpapers for Jan 2016

And if cats are your thing, you can download my freebie for January. Illustrated in watercolour, and based around my own three cats, these pattern digital wallpapers are fun and cute, with their celebration-themed elements of honeycomb paper decorations, party poppers and buckets of popcorn. They also hint at two of January's 'Holidays', Hat Day and Popcorn Day!
