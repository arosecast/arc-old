Wish List // 6 Places to Buy Lovely Notebooks & Notepads

Published // 20 July, 2016  |  Column // Wish list

Who else is a stationery addict? I have so many notebooks, but still lust after buying even more. I should actually just sit down and use the ones I have so I can occasionally treat myself to a replacement, but I always have a hard time marking their pretty pages.

Here are a few of my favourites notebooks at the moment. These wonderful designers and illustrators are inspiring me to design a few notebooks for my own products.

1. Notebooks by Papio Press

From foxes and bull dogs to unicorns and tigers, Papio Press have a massive range of absolutely gorgeous notebooks. I've bought from them before, and the creators behind the company are just incredibly lovely as well.

2. Notebooks by OH NO RACHIO!

I love Rachel's work (I treated myself to her constellation moon phase enamel pin a little while back) and I think these kraft notebooks with white ink illustrations and lettering are really beautiful pieces.

3. Cat Inspired Notebooks from ilikecats

I have a hard time not including cats somewhere in my posts. I've loved the stuff produced by Toby of ilikecats for a long time, and these notebooks are no exception.

4. Grand Budapest Hotel Notebook by FamilyTree

I'm a massive Wes Anderson fan, and when The Grand Budapest Hotel came out, it felt like he had made a movie that gathered all my greatest loves together into one celluloid dream. So I would love this little notebook to carry around.

5. Notepads on Etsy by Quill and Fox

These quaint illustrated notepads and to-do lists from Quill and Fox come in a few different themes, from flowers and plants, to animals and this little book club version. They all evoke a welcome sense of nostalgia and a quiet softness.

6. Library To Do Desk Notepad by Idlewild

One of many ways to make me lust after a product is to give a library theme. This deskplanner, with illustrations of a globe, books and indoor plants, is adorable. It’s sized to sit on your desk, and, with 52 sheets, it helps you plan the whole year out (like my vintage glasses weekly planner.)

My Work // Malcolm 'Mal' Reynolds of Joss Whedon's Firefly | Watercolour & Pencil Portrait Illustration

Published // 18 July, 2016  |  Column // My work

I’ve started doing a few flashbacks posts talking about some of my favourite pieces from my back-catalogue; first up was my fan art illustration of Willow Rosenberg from Joss Whedon’s Buffy The Vampire Slayer; then I shared Captain Jean-Luc Picard from Star Trek: The Next Generation. Today, it’s all about another fan art piece, this time of Malcolm ‘Mal’ Reynolds of Joss Whedon’s classic sci-fi/western Firefly.

I’m a big nerd; you can see that from a number of my wish list posts. I came to Firefly a little late. I had heard about it around the time it was first released, but knowing it had been suddenly cancelled, I didn’t look too much into it. A few years later, its popularity had exploded, especially with the release of the movie. So I rented the first DVD to check it out. I don’t think I got around to renting the second DVD as I went straight to buying the box set.

It’s an immense shame to see such a great show cut down just when it had barely begun. But despite having only 14 episodes and a movie, it is one of the most respected sci-fi shows of the past few decades due to its mixing of varied genres, flawed characters and stellar writing.

So I felt I should do a little ode to the show and one of its greatest characters, Malcolm ‘Mal’ Reynolds, brilliantly portrayed by Nathan Fillion. And just like Willow and Picard, I was really ecstatic with how he turned out.

Buy this Piece on Society6, Art Rookie,
Redbubble or Etsy

I’ve popped A4 and A6 art prints of Mal up on my Etsy store, along with a greeting card version with the additional banner of text, "You Little Rebel You". It makes for a great greeting card to give to a nerdy friend.

I currently have five fan art illustrations in this line of art prints and greeting cards. Malcolm ‘Mal’ Reynolds is joined by; Captain Picard from the sci-fi epic Star Trek: The Next Generation; Willow Rosenberg from Joss Whedon’s Buffy The Vampire Slayer; Usagi Tsukino from the manga/anime Sailor Moon; and Princess Bubblegum from the animated show Adventure Time (though she still needs the greeting card treatment).

And like most of my products, you can also buy the Captain Jean-Luc Picard illustration on a number different products over on my Society6, Redbubble and Art Rookie stores, from cushions and cards to mugs and rugs.
